Dunki 2023

Dunki 2023

  • Duration : 2h 40m
  • Country : India
  • Actors : Shah Rukh Khan, Taapsee Pannu, Vicky Kaushal, Boman Irani

Movie Details

The latest release from Shah Rukh Khan (SRK), Dunki is not a massy action flick like his last two releases. The Rajkumar Hirani film Dunki tells the story of a group of friends who decide to go to England to make money. However, their dreams are crushed due to stringent visa regulations. The group of friends decided to take the IELTS exam to get a Student Visa to England. Things take a chaotic turn and the group is forced to take the illegal route outside the country that changes the rest of their lives. Dunki is patriotic and emotional through and through. 

This potboiler follows the life of a soldier who owes his life to a stranger. After learning about the situation of the stranger’s family, this soldier devotes his entire life to helping them and thus becomes a messiah they didn’t know they needed. The story of this film is simple yet layered with feelings which makes it a tear-jerker for any viewer living in the diaspora. The harsh reality of living away from home and the fact that migrants are never fully welcomed in the countries they flee to is presented realistically in Dunki.

The film has some hilarious dialogue, most of which come in the form of broken English that is spoken by the Punjabi aspirants who wish to live in England. However, it is the introspective one-liners that make you want to stop and look back at the plight and sacrifice of the migrants; the people who moved away from their homes to make new homes in lands that did not fully welcome them.The crux of Dunki is its characters. Aside from Shah Rukh Khan whose emotional performance is superior, Taapsee Pannu does a great job as his leading lady despite their 22-year age gap. The other main characters also do a great job at their particular characters but it is Vicky Kaushal who steals the show with his cameo appearance. While one wishes to see more of Shah Rukh Khan and Vicky Kaushal together, what we get in Dunki is commendable. The original soundtrack is tear-jerking as well with ‘Nikle The Kabhi Jo’, a song that narrates the plight of immigrants who long for their own homes. Other songs like ‘O Maahi’ and ‘Lut Put Gaya’ serve as romantic tracks which make you swoon. One complaint that some viewers may have with the film is that Dunki focuses less on the journey that took these immigrants across borders and more on the reasons behind their journey as well as the consequences of the said “Donkey” journey. However, all things considered, Dunki narrates an emotional, gut-wrenching story of the reality of diaspora.
